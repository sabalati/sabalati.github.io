About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Sapienza University of Rome (2021–2026). My thesis focuses on a semantic and formal encoding of Object-Centric Event Data (OCED), with applications to compliance checking, monitoring, and verification over object-centric event logs and knowledge graphs.

My research interests include Object-Centric Process Mining, formal methods, formal verification, satisfiability/constraint solving, runtime verification, and semantic technologies (RDF/OWL, SPARQL). I also work on applied domains such as IoT, smart systems, and smart cities.

Research

Object-Centric Event Data (OCED)

Developed formal and semantic reference implementations of the OCED meta-model (IEEE), including knowledge-graph encodings and tooling to support reasoning, monitoring, and compliance checking over object-centric event data.

Verification & Compliance

Focus on temporal logic, constraint solving, and Alloy-driven verification to check behavioral properties and compliance constraints, bridging formal specifications with object-centric logs and knowledge graphs.

Smart Systems

Formal modeling and verification of IoT and blockchain-enabled smart systems (e.g., smart city components) using methods such as TLA+ and other formal languages.
